
In the ancient world of Arenfall, legends were carved into mountains and remembered by the stars themselves.
The people whispered the name of Aren across ruined kingdoms, frozen battlefields, forgotten temples, and dark oceans.
Far beyond the horizon, the shadow of Void Serpent stretched across the world like an endless storm.



CHAPTER 1



Forgotten ruins beneath the mountains contained weapons forged before human civilization existed.

The wind carried the sound of distant drums across the valleys of Arenfall. Ancient castles stood on cliffs above endless forests while ravens circled ruined towers.
Travelers spoke of secret alliances, hidden artifacts, forgotten gods, and monsters that wandered beneath the earth after sunset.
In crowded taverns, survivors described armies marching beneath black banners while dragons crossed the skies like living storms.

Aren remembered every battle, every betrayal, and every fallen companion.
Some nights were filled with silence and sorrow. Other nights exploded into violence as blades clashed beneath thunder and fire.
Kings argued over power while ordinary people struggled simply to survive another winter.

Far away, deep inside the dark citadel of Void Serpent, strange rituals illuminated giant halls built from obsidian stone.
Priests wearing bone masks chanted beside rivers of fire while ancient creatures slept beneath the fortress.
The prophecy of the end had already begun, though most kingdoms were too divided to understand the danger approaching them.

Still, hope survived.

Rebels, scholars, mercenaries, exiled princes, wandering mages, dragon riders, and forgotten heroes slowly gathered around Aren.
Each carried scars from wars that never truly ended.
Each believed the world deserved another chance.

The journey across Arenfall continued through haunted forests, frozen mountains, abandoned temples, cursed oceans, underground labyrinths, and cities consumed by shadow.
Every step brought them closer to the truth.
Every battle revealed another piece of the ancient mystery connecting gods, magic, dragons, and the rise of Void Serpent.

Yet the greatest challenge was never the monsters.

It was fear.

Fear destroyed kingdoms faster than swords ever could.
Fear turned allies into traitors and brothers into enemies.
Fear allowed darkness to spread across the world unchecked.

That was why Aren refused to surrender.

Even when castles burned.
Even when dragons fell from the sky.
Even when entire armies disappeared into darkness.

The legend of Aren became stronger with every chapter of the war.
Children repeated the stories beside candlelight while old veterans prayed the prophecy would finally end.
Some believed salvation was near.
Others believed the world was already doomed.

But destiny continued moving forward.

And somewhere beyond the mountains, the final battle waited.

Travelers spoke of secret alliances, hidden artifacts, forgotten gods, and monsters that wandered beneath the earth after sunset.
In crowded taverns, survivors described armies marching beneath black banners while dragons crossed the skies like living storms.

Aren remembered every battle, every betrayal, and every fallen companion.
Some nights were filled with silence and sorrow. Other nights exploded into violence as blades clashed beneath thunder and fire.
Kings argued over power while ordinary people struggled simply to survive another winter.

Far away, deep inside the dark citadel of Void Serpent, strange rituals illuminated giant halls built from obsidian stone.
Priests wearing bone masks chanted beside rivers of fire while ancient creatures slept beneath the fortress.
The prophecy of the end had already begun, though most kingdoms were too divided to understand the danger approaching them.

Still, hope survived.

Rebels, scholars, mercenaries, exiled princes, wandering mages, dragon riders, and forgotten heroes slowly gathered around Aren.
Each carried scars from wars that never truly ended.
Each believed the world deserved another chance.

The journey across Arenfall continued through haunted forests, frozen mountains, abandoned temples, cursed oceans, underground labyrinths, and cities consumed by shadow.
Every step brought them closer to the truth.
Every battle revealed another piece of the ancient mystery connecting gods, magic, dragons, and the rise of Void Serpent.

Yet the greatest challenge was never the monsters.

It was fear.

Fear destroyed kingdoms faster than swords ever could.
Fear turned allies into traitors and brothers into enemies.
Fear allowed darkness to spread across the world unchecked.

That was why Aren refused to surrender.

Even when castles burned.
Even when dragons fell from the sky.
Even when entire armies disappeared into darkness.

The legend of Aren became stronger with every chapter of the war.
Children repeated the stories beside candlelight while old veterans prayed the prophecy would finally end.
Some believed salvation was near.
Others believed the world was already doomed.

But destiny continued moving forward.

And somewhere beyond the mountains, the final battle waited.

Far away, deep inside the dark citadel of Void Serpent, strange rituals illuminated giant halls built from obsidian stone.
Priests wearing bone masks chanted beside rivers of fire while ancient creatures slept beneath the fortress.
The prophecy of the end had already begun, though most kingdoms were too divided to understand the danger approaching them.

Still, hope survived.

Rebels, scholars, mercenaries, exiled princes, wandering mages, dragon riders, and forgotten heroes slowly gathered around Aren.
Each carried scars from wars that never truly ended.
Each believed the world deserved another chance.

The journey across Arenfall continued through haunted forests, frozen mountains, abandoned temples, cursed oceans, underground labyrinths, and cities consumed by shadow.
Every step brought them closer to the truth.
Every battle revealed another piece of the ancient mystery connecting gods, magic, dragons, and the rise of Void Serpent.

Yet the greatest challenge was never the monsters.

It was fear.

Fear destroyed kingdoms faster than swords ever could.
Fear turned allies into traitors and brothers into enemies.
Fear allowed darkness to spread across the world unchecked.

That was why Aren refused to surrender.

Even when castles burned.
Even when dragons fell from the sky.
Even when entire armies disappeared into darkness.

The legend of Aren became stronger with every chapter of the war.
Children repeated the stories beside candlelight while old veterans prayed the prophecy would finally end.
Some believed salvation was near.
Others believed the world was already doomed.

But destiny continued moving forward.

And somewhere beyond the mountains, the final battle waited.
